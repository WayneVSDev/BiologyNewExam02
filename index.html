Biology Mock Test with Diagrams – Grade 11

Test your knowledge and learn with diagrams! Answer each question and click "Check" for feedback.

1. What component of the cell membrane gives it flexibility?

a) Proteins
b) Cholesterol
c) Phospholipid heads
d) Carbohydrate chains

2. Which molecule can cross the lipid bilayer most easily without assistance?

a) Glucose
b) Water
c) Carbon dioxide
d) Potassium ions

3. What type of transport requires energy to move substances against their gradient?

a) Simple diffusion
b) Facilitated diffusion
c) Active transport
d) Osmosis

4. Which protein type is most likely to span the entire cell membrane?

a) Peripheral proteins
b) Integral proteins
c) Surface proteins
d) Attachment proteins

5. What happens to a plant cell in a hypertonic solution?

a) It swells and bursts
b) It becomes turgid
c) It undergoes plasmolysis
d) It remains unchanged

6. Which of these is a function of carrier proteins?

a) Anchoring the cytoskeleton
b) Facilitating diffusion of specific molecules
c) Forming pores for water
d) Receiving chemical signals

7. What property of the lipid bilayer prevents charged ions from crossing easily?

a) Its hydrophilic heads
b) Its hydrophobic core
c) Its protein channels
d) Its cholesterol content

8. Which molecule provides energy for active transport?

a) Glucose
b) ATP
c) Oxygen
d) Water

9. What type of solution has the same solute concentration as the cell’s interior?

a) Hypertonic
b) Hypotonic
c) Isotonic
d) Osmotic

10. Which membrane component helps cells recognize each other?

a) Cholesterol
b) Phospholipids
c) Glycoproteins
d) Pump proteins

13. a) Explain the process of osmosis across a cell membrane. b) Predict what happens to an animal cell in an isotonic solution, including a simple diagram description. (8 marks)

14. a) Compare passive and active transport across the cell membrane. b) Describe the role of pump proteins in active transport with an example and a diagram sketch idea. (10 marks)
